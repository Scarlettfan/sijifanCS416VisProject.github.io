    There is a noticeable trend from this data set. The confirmed cases were decreasing in the first half of the year in 2021 but were increasing in the second half of the year. The reason can be caused by the Delta Variant explosion in mid 2021 across the states. According to a study led by the University of Cincinnati, “rural counties had 2.4 times more infections per 100,000 people than urban areas between July 1 and Aug. 31, 2021, when the delta variant surged across the United States.”

    Source: https://www.uc.edu/news/articles/2022/02/delta-variant-of-covid-19-hit-rural-america-especially-hard.html
